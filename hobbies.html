My hobbies are many and varying, but I will discuss just a few here.

I enjoy learning about and practicing mathematics. Since I began to teach myself calculus freshman year, I have had a penchant for doing math because I enjoy it, a fact which perplexes many people who know me. I spent the end of freshman year and the following summer learning about the fundamentals of calculus, sophomore year deepening my knowledge with a much more advanced textbook, and midway through junior year, I discovered the Putnam Exam, or more properly, the William Lowell Putnam Mathematical Competition. I was stunned by the sheer difficulty of the problems, but nonetheless, I worked hard to discover the special secret to each one as I tried to solve it. While not always successful in solving the problem, I often found the path to the answer, and was always excited by the elegance of the method with which the problem could be solved. Now, I have begun to once again attempt these sorts of problems, and I hope to expand my problem solving abilities in doing so.

I enjoy trying to learn how to produce music. As discussed on my music page, I am a fan of psytrance. Now, I am try to make it. I have spent hundreds of dollars of gift money on software and have received gifts such as studio-grade headphones to contribute to the hobby. Ideally, I would like to make it something I can do at a professional level, but it will never be something I can dedicate all of my time to (something which can be said of any other of my hobbies, to be fair).

Other hobbies I enjoy include theology and political philosophy, but I discuss these on my beliefs page.
