Religion

I have been a Bible-believing Christian my entire life, coming from an originally Reformed background. In recent years, my faith has become very important to me, to the point where I attempt to make everything else in my life connect back to it, and if I cannot, to analyze whether it is something which really should be a part of my life at all. My parents are now Calvinistic Dispensationalist Baptists, which was my leaning for a long time, until recently. I have been carefully considering Reformed theology and Lutheran theology. While most theology has no bearing on whether one is saved or not, it does provide important insights on how to live rightly, how to worship God, and how to view various aspects of the Christian faith. Reformed theology is based off of the works of John Calvin, who took the Reformation even further than Martin Luther. Thus it is very Calvinistic in nature, in favor of paedobaptism, and focused on the doctrines of grace. Lutheranism is similar to Calvinism in some ways, but is distinguished by some differences between the "5 points of Calvinism" and Lutheran positions on the issues which the five points address. Lutheranism, in contrast to Reformed theology, holds that Christ is physically present during communion, and that baptism is a means of God's grace.

Politics

I was, at one time someone who would have identified as "conservative". While my views on morality could still be described as such, the word no longer aptly describes my political views. I am a libertarian, and in particuar, a Christian anarcho-capitalist. This means that I oppose the existence of the State for reasons related to my religion and philosophy, and that I believe that markets should be free with no State intervention. I do not believe that the State should intervene in the affairs of adults provided both parties engage in transactions voluntarily. This immediately excludes the possibility of taxation, since taxation requires physical force. Many object and say "BUT WHO WOULD BUILD THE ROADS?", citing other similar objections related to the military and other public services. The simple response is this: private organizations would build the roads, funded by voluntary payments rather than taxation. Military organizations would be voluntarily in a similar manner. All other services provided by government are immoral redistribution schemes which rely on theft instead of charity to help the poor, which I claim is radically harmful to the poor and far less efficient than private organizations, especially churches.

Overall, Christian anarcho-capitalism is anti-war, pro-free-markets, anti-State, pro-life, anti-Drug-War, pro-gun, and pro-civil-liberties.
